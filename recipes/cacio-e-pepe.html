Cacio e Pepe

Description

The basis of all Roman pasta dishes, cacio e pepe, is a simple primo piatto consisting of only three main ingredients: pasta, Pecorino Romano, and pepper, with pasta cooking water being the crucial fourth ingredient that's essential to make the sauce. However, although at first glance, a simple dish to make, the technique may take some practice to perfect, since the sauce has to be just right — that is, creamy, silky, and luscious. The secret lies in concentrating starch by cooking pasta in a small amount of water, which is then used to dilute the Pecorino Romano before it's stirred, off heat, together with pasta, ground pepper, and the rest of the water. As a finishing touch, once plated, and before serving, all that is required is a light sprinkling of pepper and, optionally, grated pecorino.

Ingredients:

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 180g Pecorino Romano
  • 8 pinches of roasted and crushed pepper
  • Salt, as needed

Steps:

  1. Roast the peppercorns in a nonstick pan over medium heat, until they start turning translucent.
  2. Then, place the peppercorns on a paper towel to cool. Once cooled, crush them in a mortar and pestle or with the back of a pan, so you end up with pepper that is fine as a powder but interspersed with larger specks of pepper.
  3. Bring a pot of water to a boil, then salt it and throw in the spaghetti to cook until al dente — ideally, boil the spaghetti in a smaller amount of water so you end up with cooking water that has more starch which will help make a creamier sauce.
  4. Add the Pecorino Romano to an aluminum pan, then add four ladlefuls of the hot cooking water, mixing it as you add.
  5. Once the cheese has melted, add the pepper, and add the pasta (take it out of the water using a carving fork or thongs, so there is still water left on the spaghetti) and mix until the sauce coats the spaghetti.
  6. Serve immediately, on a hot plate, sprinkled with pepper and Pecorino Romano.
